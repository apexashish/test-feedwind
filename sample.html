A year ago, if someone had told me words like "gamified learning", "growth mind set", "entrepreneurship", "reflective learning", "peer learning", and "peer evaluation", I would have thought they are parroting what they just heard in some multi-national HR event or an MBA program.

After my daughter joined Sparkling Mindz, I came to see each of these buzz words put to real and effective use. At Sparkling Mindz, everything they do is deeply connected to their value system.

Children learn to take responsibility for their learning there by becoming life long learners. Children take responsibility for their's peers learning too - this build deeper understanding as well ability to work in larger communities/teams. Sparkling Mindz focuses on deep and connected learning - their understanding is interdisciplinary.

The subject names are little non-standard at Sparkling Mindz which I now realize why:
- Math is about building analytical thinking, problem solving, and design thinking
- Languages are about developing rich communication skills
- Sience employs a lot enquery
- Social sciences (history, civics, ...) takes a holistic view of self, community and the world. Critical thinking is nurtured to make sense of the complex world.

All of these employs peer evaluation and reflection - it accelerates and deepens the learning.

Does that sound like an alternative school for people who don't care about the board exams? Not really. The school strives for a very high academic standards. While the first batch has not yet appeared for public exam, I'm quite confident they will do really well in exams as well.

By now you must be wondering how much I get paid to write this. Well, I pay the school and not the other way :) I'm super impressed about them.

They have open houses once in a while. Thats a great place to experiance the school before you take the leap.

Deciding on which playschool to put our son in turned out to be a much bigger decision than we had anticipated. “Pre-school years are the most critical as they set the tone for how your child learns to do life” we were told and so we delved into trying to find the right school for our son. We explored various options and visited several schools before our friends recommended Sparkling Mindz. We had several apprehensions with this school – the distance from our home, the environment, the fact that it was in a commercial building were some. Before visiting different schools, I made a checklist in consultation with two experienced early childhood development professionals in the family who told me that if I was able to check 7 out of 10 things on my list at any school, we should seriously consider that one. Sparkling Mindz was the only one that stood out from this exercise we’d done. Our first conversation with the team blew us away. I’m amazed at how there’s so much focus on training and equipping the facilitators (teachers). I love how invested they are in my son. I was surprised at how quickly they were able to understand my son’s personality and the type of learner he is. They’ve worked with us on behaviours we’d like to instill in our child, for example he went through a phase of hitting people. We saw a remarkable difference in this behaviour after it was addressed at school – they inspired a change in his behaviour.

As parents, it’s incredibly difficult to trust people with your child, especially people you don’t know very well people and people that play a big role in shaping your child. The SM team really gained our trust (and quickly) by showing us time and again how invested they are in our son and how seriously they take their jobs. I’m convinced that we will keep looking back on this decision for years to come and pat ourselves on our backs for getting this right.
